# CDNIS Interview Coaching for Angela

## Overview
- Participants: Coach (SPK_A) and Angela (SPK_B), located in Guangzhou.
- Purpose: Prepare Angela for her CDNIS Grade 7 interview with practical strategies, tailored answers, and research tasks.
- Tone and Approach: Supportive, pragmatic coaching focused on aligning Angela’s strengths with CDNIS’s culture and offerings, and on making a positive, likable impression.

## Key Guidance and Talking Points

### Managing Nerves and Mindset
- A little stress can improve performance; too much hurts it. Aim for a calm, prepared state.[idx-33]
- Interviewers want to say “yes”; make it easy by being likable, specific, and well-researched.

### School Knowledge to Demonstrate
- Explore Academics → Upper School → Transition Years (Grades 6–8) on the CDNIS site; learn courses and sports, especially anything not at your current school.[idx-30]
- Watch recent, relevant CDNIS videos (e.g., Grades 6–11 webinars, campus tour).[idx-29]
- Culture:
  - Daily assembly includes singing the Canadian national anthem (“O Canada”).[idx-46]
  - House system exists (connect with your current “White House” experience).
  - Student artwork is displayed across campus.
  - Sports: Strong in basketball; show willingness to try Canadian favorites like ice hockey.[idx-44]
  - Canada–France link: Many Canadians speak French (e.g., Quebec).[idx-163]

### “Tell us about yourself”
- Keep it concise and distinctive.
- Include something positive about your current school (SA School): kind classmates, good environment, supportive teachers.
  - Best teacher example: Art teacher Mr. Cao—highlight his strong own artwork and good preparation (avoid trivial details like buying milk tea/burgers).[idx-24]
- You’re exploring CDNIS for a new, different experience, not because you dislike your current school.

### “What kind of student are you?”
- Avoid “hardworking” (too common, hard to prove).
- Use: balanced (across subjects), sociable and approachable, generally well-liked by peers and teachers.[idx-74]
- Emphasize: you readily ask for help and admit when you don’t understand (comfort communicating with teachers).[idx-132]

### Teamwork and Leadership
- Start with listening, sharing ideas, comparing viewpoints to reach a good answer.[idx-89]
- How to lead naturally:
  - Volunteer for extra responsibilities (note-taking, organizing, presenting). “The person with the pencil” often becomes the leader.[idx-99]
  - Resolve disputes with action: offer to do more work to move the group forward.
  - Show flexibility: you don’t need to lead every time; you’re willing to let others lead and lead next time.[idx-103]

### Weakness Question (Reframing)
- Academic: Music (last term B; others A).[idx-105]
- Personality/approach: Avoid “careless.” Use:
  - “Sometimes I rush” or
  - “I over-schedule because I want to participate more,” which can lead to disorganization—then note how you’re improving.

### Overcoming a Challenge
- MC example: First time hosting a Monday ceremony—felt nervous but took the first step and succeeded; continued serving as MC afterward.[idx-116]
- Calming tactic: Look at empty seats/fixed points instead of faces.
- Meaning: The school trusted you with responsibility; demonstrates resilience and public-speaking ability.

### Contributions and Extracurriculars
- Be specific about competitions and clubs (learn exact CDNIS names):
  - Math: Maths Olympiad
  - English: Debate
  - Science/Tech: Robotics, etc.
- Sports: Mention basketball experience; interest in trying ice hockey.[idx-45]
- Avoid jumping straight to “student council” before knowing the school—focus first on current clubs and teams.

### Hobbies and Interests (Interview-Friendly)
- Choose hobbies that support your academic/character profile:
  - Drawing/art.
  - Watching travel videos; traveled to Canada, Japan, Denmark (Copenhagen), France, etc.[idx-155]
- Avoid mentioning video games, excessive mobile phone use, or AI as hobbies unless tied to an achievement.

### Favorite Book/Media
- Book: The Secret Garden (prepare a concise plot summary and core message; check if there’s a film version you’ve seen).[idx-190]
- Be ready to connect themes (growth, healing, friendship) to your own values.

### End-of-Interview Questions (Use “if” to show humility)
- “If I join CDNIS, how do you support new students in adjusting socially and academically?”[idx-176]
- “If I join, what common mistakes do new students make, and how can I avoid them?”[idx-176]
- “If I join, how does CDNIS promote Canadian culture (e.g., French language exposure, cultural activities, sports like ice hockey)?”[idx-181]
- Avoid questions you could have answered by reading the website.

## Phrasing Tips and Pitfalls to Avoid
- Replace “hardworking” with “balanced, approachable, sociable, well-liked.”
- Replace “careless” with “I sometimes rush/over-schedule.”[idx-112]
- Avoid mentioning video games/mobile phones/AI unless linked to notable accomplishments.
- Be specific: name exact clubs/teams; refer to recent CDNIS content.
- Use “if” when asking end-of-interview questions to avoid sounding presumptuous.
